**The mathematical foundation upon which the entire pipeline is built.** 
S0 is not an algorithmic step — it is the collection of **physical and geometric measurement functions** that map the 3D continuous world into the discrete pixels and inertial samples the filter consumes. Every residual, state propagation step, and EKF update Jacobian in the system is built on top of S0. 

A bug in S0 (such as a coordinate system flip or a sign error in the Jacobian) does not fail loudly with an exception. Instead, it quietly poisons every downstream stage, manifesting as mysterious, untraceable quadratic drift or immediate filter divergence.

## 1. Camera Projection Models

A camera model defines the mapping:
$$	ext{project}(\mathbf{p}_c) ightarrow \mathbf{z} = [u, v]^T$$
where $\mathbf{p}_c = [x, y, z]^T \in \mathbb{R}^3$ is a 3D point in the camera-frame coordinate system. Cortex supports both the standard **Pinhole-RadTan** model (for rectilinear lenses) and the **Kannala-Brandt** model (for wide-angle fisheye lenses).

### A. Pinhole Projection (The Core Perspective)
The ideal pinhole camera projects the 3D camera-frame coordinate $\mathbf{p}_c$ to the normalized image plane coordinates $\mathbf{x}_n = [x_n, y_n]^T$:
$$x_n = \frac{x}{z}, \quad y_n = \frac{y}{z}$$

These are then mapped to pixel coordinates via focal lengths ($f_x, f_y$) and principal points ($c_x, c_y$):
$$u = f_x x_n + c_x, \quad v = f_y y_n + c_y$$

### B. Distortion Models (Modeling Lens Curvature)
Real lenses bend light, introducing distortion. Before mapping to pixels, the normalized coordinates $\mathbf{x}_n$ are warped to distorted coordinates $\mathbf{x}_d = [x_d, y_d]^T$.

#### 1. Radial-Tangential (RadTan) Distortion (8-Parameters)
Used for standard lenses. It models barrel/pincushion radial distortion (parameters $k_1, k_2, k_3$) and tangential decentering distortion (parameters $p_1, p_2$):
$$r^2 = x_n^2 + y_n^2$$
$$x_d = x_n (1 + k_1 r^2 + k_2 r^4 + k_3 r^6) + 2p_1 x_n y_n + p_2 (r^2 + 2x_n^2)$$
$$y_d = y_n (1 + k_1 r^2 + k_2 r^4 + k_3 r^6) + p_1 (r^2 + 2y_n^2) + 2p_2 x_n y_n$$

#### 2. Kannala-Brandt (KB) Fisheye Distortion (4-Parameters)
Used for extreme wide-angle wide-field-of-view lenses. It models distortion as an expansion of the angle $	heta$ relative to the optical axis:
$$r = \sqrt{x_n^2 + y_n^2}, \quad 	heta = 	ext{atan2}(r, 1)$$
$$d(	heta) = 	heta + k_1 	heta^3 + k_2 	heta^5 + k_3 	heta^7 + k_4 	heta^9$$
$$x_d = \frac{d(	heta)}{r} x_n, \quad y_d = \frac{d(	heta)}{r} y_n$$

### C. The Analytical Projection Jacobian
The EKF update requires the derivative of pixel coordinates $\mathbf{z} = [u, v]^T$ w.r.t the 3D camera-frame point $\mathbf{p}_c$:
$$\mathbf{H}_p = \frac{\partial \mathbf{z}}{\partial \mathbf{p}_c} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{z}}{\partial \mathbf{x}_d} \cdot \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}_d}{\partial \mathbf{x}_n} \cdot \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}_n}{\partial \mathbf{p}_c}$$

The first-order linear perspective term is given by:
$$\frac{\partial \mathbf{x}_n}{\partial \mathbf{p}_c} = \begin{bmatrix} 1/z & 0 & -x/z^2 \ 0 & 1/z & -y/z^2 \end{bmatrix}$$

Cortex checks the rank of $\mathbf{H}_p$ (which must be strictly 2) and validates its analytic derivatives against numerical finite differences during unit testing down to **machine precision** ($\approx10^{-15}$).

## 2. Inertial Sensor (IMU) Physics

An Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) consists of a 3-axis gyroscope and a 3-axis accelerometer. The continuous-time physical models are:

$$	ilde{\boldsymbol{\omega}}(t) = \boldsymbol{\omega}(t) + \mathbf{b}_g(t) + \mathbf{n}_g(t)$$
$$	ilde{\mathbf{a}}(t) = \mathbf{R}_{W}^{I}(t) \left( \mathbf{a}_W(t) - \mathbf{g}_W ight) + \mathbf{b}_a(t) + \mathbf{n}_a(t)$$

Where:
*   $	ilde{\boldsymbol{\omega}}, 	ilde{\mathbf{a}}$: The raw gyro and accelerometer measurements.
*   $\boldsymbol{\omega}, \mathbf{a}_W$: The true angular velocity and true world-frame acceleration of the IMU.
*   $\mathbf{b}_g, \mathbf{b}_a$: The slow-varying **sensor biases** (modeled as random walks).
*   $\mathbf{n}_g, \mathbf{n}_a$: White Gaussian measurement noise.
*   $\mathbf{g}_W$: The global gravity vector. 

### Why an IMU Resting on a Table Reads $+9.81	ext{ m/s}^2$
This is a common point of confusion for beginners. Accelerometers measure **specific force** (the reaction force relative to free-fall), not true acceleration. 
When an IMU is stationary on a flat table:
*   Its true world acceleration is $\mathbf{a}_W = \mathbf{0}$.
*   The world gravity is $\mathbf{g}_W = [0, 0, -9.81]^T$.
*   Thus, the accelerometer reads:
    $$	ilde{\mathbf{a}} = \mathbf{R}_W^I (\mathbf{0} - [0, 0, -9.81]^T) = \mathbf{R}_W^I [0, 0, 9.81]^T$$
*   This registers as a $+9.81	ext{ m/s}^2$ **upward** acceleration. A VIO pipeline must subtract gravity during propagation to prevent the drone from quadratically shooting off into the sky in its state estimate.

## 3. Space-Time Calibration

Sensors do not live at the same physical point, nor do they capture data at the exact same instant. We must calibrate both spatial and temporal offsets.

### A. Spatial Extrinsics (6-DoF Calibration)
The camera is offset from the IMU center by a translation $\mathbf{p}_{ic}$ and a rotation $\mathbf{R}_{ic}$ (or $\mathbf{T}_{ci} \in 	ext{SE}(3)$). A point $\mathbf{p}_f$ in the world frame is mapped to the camera frame $C$ via the intermediate IMU frame $I$:
$$\mathbf{p}_{imu} = \mathbf{R}_{W}^{I} (\mathbf{p}_f - \mathbf{p}_{W\_imu})$$
$$\mathbf{p}_{cam} = \mathbf{R}_{ic}^T (\mathbf{p}_{imu} - \mathbf{p}_{ic})$$

Under dynamic motion, omitting even a $1	ext{ cm}$ offset introduces massive unmodeled **centripetal and Euler accelerations** into the camera's visual track:
$$\mathbf{a}_{cam} = \mathbf{a}_{imu} + \boldsymbol{\omega} 	imes (\boldsymbol{\omega} 	imes \mathbf{p}_{ic}) + \dot{\boldsymbol{\omega}} 	imes \mathbf{p}_{ic}$$

### B. Temporal Extrinsics (Time-Offset Calibration)
Due to transmission latency, clock drift, or rolling shutters, there is a time delay $t_d$ between the camera clock and the IMU clock:
$$t_{camera} = t_{imu} - t_d$$

If the drone is rotating at a moderate angular velocity of $100^\circ	ext{/s}$ (common in maneuvers), a tiny **$5	ext{ millisecond}$** unmodeled time offset ($t_d = 0.005$) introduces a **$0.5^\circ$** alignment error. This is equivalent to several pixels of visual tracking error, causing the EKF update to inject false corrections.

## The S0 Contract

The contract defines the required behavior of the sensor and calibration mathematical block:

| Element | Contract Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Signature** | `project(p_c, ζ) → (u,v)`; `imu_model(ω, a, R, g, b, n)`; `T_ci ∈ SE(3)`; `t_d ∈ ℝ` |
| **Pre-conditions** | Focal lengths $f_x, f_y > 0$; distortion parameters are within stable convergence bounds; gravity vector orientation is frame-consistent. |
| **Post-conditions & Invariants** | **1. Round-Trip consistency**: `unproject(project(p_c)) ≈ p_c / ‖p_c‖` down to $10^{-15}$ machine precision for all depth $z > 0$.<br/>**2. Jacobian rank**: Projection Jacobian $\mathbf{H}_p$ is strictly rank-2 and matches numerical finite-differences.<br/>**3. Cheirality**: Points behind the lens ($z \le 0$) are rejected, never projected.<br/>**4. Inertial Identity**: A zero-noise, gravity-compensated stationary IMU integrates to exactly zero velocity and zero translation over time. |

## Real-Data Validation via `s0_inspect`

Cortex includes a real-data inspector tool, **`s0_inspect`**, that runs the projection and IMU models on actual EuRoC sensor streams to verify physical calibration.

```bash
# Execute the real-data sensor inspector on EuRoC V1_01_easy
./build/sitl-debug/tools/s0_inspect --dataset /path/to/V1_01_easy/mav0 --out build/s0 --frame 0

# Compile the visualization overlays
node docs-site/scripts/gen-overlay.mjs build/s0
```

This generates two critical diagnostic overlays:

### A. Camera Model Overlay (`distortion.svg`)
Superimposes an ideal, distortion-free rectilinear grid (grey) against the actual lens-warped grid (green) calculated from the distortion coefficients. It draws lens displacement vectors and verifies the round-trip projection error across every node of the grid. This allows you to **visually inspect and confirm** that the lens model is perfectly inverting the camera's physical barrel distortion.

### B. IMU Allan Deviation Curve (`imu_allan.svg`)
Computes the **Allan Deviation** of the gyroscope and accelerometer streams under static conditions. The Allan deviation curve (plotted on a log-log scale) allows you to extract:
1.  **White Noise Density ($N$)**: Sourced from the slope of $-0.5$ at $t = 1	ext{ s}$. This represents the continuous-time noise parameters used in the process noise covariance $\mathbf{Q}$.
2.  **Bias Instability ($B$)**: Sourced from the flat bottom of the curve.
3.  **Random Walk ($K$)**: Sourced from the slope of $+0.5$ at long integration times.

Running `s0_inspect` on the physical drone rig allows you to verify that your configured process noises exactly match the true physical noise profile of the sensor, protecting the filter from initial over-confidence.
